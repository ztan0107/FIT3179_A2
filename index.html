Natural Disaster

Natural Disaster refer to the negative impacts that follow the occurrence of natural hazards, particularly when they cause significant harm to communities.

Malaysia is fortunate to be in a geologically stable region, free from severe natural disasters like earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, and typhoons, though it does experience floods, landslides, and severe haze. In this exploration, we will look at how extreme natural disasters affect other countries around the world that are more vulnerable to such events.

Number of Disaster Occur

Over the years, the occurrence of natural disasters has increased significantly, largely due to human activity. Global warming is causing temperatures to rise, which in turn impacts climates around the world.

Floods, in particular, have become more frequent, especially in high-income countries. This is largely due to urbanization, such as the construction of buildings, highways, and parking lots, as well as deforestation. Which Natural Disaster contributes to Deaths?

There are many factors and conditions that cause natural disasters, but Earthquake consistently contribute to significant loss of life due to their intensity. They have the power to topple buildings and bridges, rupture gas pipelines and other infrastructure, and trigger secondary disasters like landslides, tsunamis, and volcanic eruptions.

Does that affects the GDP?

As natural disasters occur more frequently, they significantly impact a country's GDP by damaging infrastructure, disrupting businesses and industries, reducing productivity, increasing government spending, and affecting trade and investment.

We observe that smaller countries tend to suffer more severe economic damage from natural disasters. This is often because they are located in disaster-prone areas, such as islands, and their economies are heavily reliant on a single sector or resource.
